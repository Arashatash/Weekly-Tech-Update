Capital & Theses

5 items
Services Are The New Software

Sequoia's hardened 2026 thesis: for every $1 spent on software, $6 is spent on services, and AI-native 'autopilots' that sell completed work (closed books, settled tickets, drafted contracts) — not tools — will capture a ~$10T market. Implication for capital: copilot SaaS is structurally short, vertical autopilots with outcome pricing are structurally long, and incumbents face an innovator's-dilemma trap if they cannibalize their tool revenue.

Sequoia Capital services-thesis autopilots vertical-ai
2026 Is The Year of Agents

At AI Ascent 2026, Sonya Huang declared agents have crossed from useless to production-grade because models, tools, and 'harnesses' (orchestration) finally converged; Pat Grady framed today's state as functionally indistinguishable from AGI for commercial purposes. Implication: capital is rotating from pure-model bets to harness/orchestration layers and to companies exploiting the 'diffusion gap' between frontier capability and Fortune 500 deployment.

Sequoia Capital agents orchestration diffusion-gap
Open Source AI As National Strategy

Ben Horowitz's argument: the US lost the open-source AI cultural layer to China via DeepSeek and similar models that now run inside US enterprises and labs; closed-model policy locked American innovation behind walls while Beijing-aligned weights spread globally. Implication: a16z is positioning for decentralized/open-weight AI infra, sovereign-AI plays, and US-domiciled open-source labs as a policy-aligned investable wedge.

a16z open-source geopolitics policy
Compute Is The Binding Constraint

Brockman (OpenAI), echoed by Sequoia and DeepMind, frames 2026 as compute-scarce: capability scales with compute, GPU availability 'rounds to zero,' and memory is now ~two-thirds of AI chip BOM. Implication: capital flows are bifurcating toward compute (data centers, memory, custom silicon, power) and toward token-efficient architectures, while application-layer founders must design for cost-per-token volatility.

Y Combinator compute infra memory
AI Search & Brand Visibility As A New Category

Berlin's Peec more than doubled ARR to $10M in months helping brands monitor and steer their presence inside AI answers — proof that 'GEO/AEO' (generative engine optimization) is becoming a defensible SaaS category as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity displace blue-link search. Implication: a near-term enterprise budget shift from SEO/PR analytics to AI-visibility tooling, with European challengers credibly leading.
