WEEK 21 · May 18 – May 24, 2026

Foothills of the singularity meet a permanent price war: capital pivots from frontier models to the agents, memory and open-weight infrastructure underneath them.

This week Google I/O's 'foothills of the singularity' framing collided with DeepSeek's decision to make a 75% V4-Pro price cut permanent, while Sequoia's AI Ascent essays declared services - not software - as the real $10T AI TAM. The combined message to operators and investors is that the frontier-model layer is commoditizing on price even as agentic capability accelerates, and the alpha is shifting to the infrastructure (memory, routing, compute financing), the distribution layer (AEO, wearables, vertical SaaS-replacement), and the policy fight over who controls open weights.

Capital & Theses

5 items
Services Are the New Software

Sequoia is reframing AI's TAM from the ~$650B software market to the ~$10T services economy: agents that deliver outcomes (legal, accounting, support, wealth) rather than seats. Implication: pricing shifts from per-seat SaaS to per-outcome/per-action, defensibility lives in workflow + proprietary data, and incumbents whose moat is human labor margins are now in the strike zone.

Open-Source AI as Geopolitical Stack

Horowitz argues the US is losing the open-source AI race to China (DeepSeek lineage), and that whoever controls the default open weights controls the encoded values of billions of devices. Capital implication: a16z is positioning portfolio bets around US open-weight labs, sovereign compute, and the policy fight to legalize broad open release. DeepSeek's permanent 75% V4-Pro price cut this week is the live ammunition for that thesis.

a16z open-source geopolitics policy
Foothills-of-Singularity Compute Buildout

Sequoia ("2026: This is AGI") and Hassabis at I/O are aligning the narrative that agentic capability is now real and the binding constraint is compute, memory, and power. Capital is moving into HBM, advanced packaging, nuclear/grid, and orchestration layers ("harnesses") rather than another foundation-model bet. With memory now ~2/3 of AI chip BOM (Epoch), the alpha is in the picks-and-shovels around the frontier model layer.

AI-Native Visibility & Distribution

Peec hitting $10M ARR in months tracking brand presence inside AI answers signals a new category: SEO is being replaced by 'AI visibility' / answer-engine optimization. Vertical AI Eating Regulated Services

The wedge: AI-native workflow + compliance + proprietary data, sold to incumbents as a services-replacement rather than a tool.
