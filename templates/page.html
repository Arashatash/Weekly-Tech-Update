TL;DR What this is & how it's built

A weekly AI strategy briefing — not a news digest — for operators and investors. Every Thursday it pulls from 7 frontier sources, hands the raw stories to Claude Opus + web search, and ships a structured report covering capital theses, what's being built, and opportunities now / mid / long.

Resources we read each week
TechCrunch AI deals, launches, regulatory news a16z Investor essays & theses Sequoia Investor essays & theses Y Combinator Founder & HN front-page pulse MIT Tech Review Research-to-product context Product Hunt What's shipping right now HF Papers Frontier ML research
How we synthesise them
A · INPUT Each Thursday, scrape up to ~140 articles from the 7 sources (last 7 days) into one corpus of raw titles, summaries, and URLs.
B · ANALYSE Claude Opus + web search filters out non-AI noise, validates URLs, and writes the briefing: capital theses first, then aligns Product Hunt launches to those theses, pulls leader voices via web search, and groups opportunities by horizon.
C · AUDIT An automated audit checks https URLs, AI-relevance, schema, horizons, source whitelist, PH thesis alignment. If anything critical fails, the briefing is re-generated with the audit feedback before it ever publishes.
D · OUTPUT Render to HTML + Markdown + JSON, archive the JSON, and deploy to GitHub Pages.
The 3-step pipeline
Step 1 · Scan 7 sources, AI-only TechCrunch · a16z · Sequoia · Y Combinator · MIT Tech Review · Product Hunt · HF Papers.
→
Step 2 · Analyse Claude Opus + web search Filter noise · validate URLs · align PH to theses · pull leader voices.
→
Step 3 · Brief 5 lenses + signals Capital theses · what's being built · opp now / mid / long · top signals · leader voices · follow the money.
Cadence: weekly · Thu 22:00 UTC Audit-gated output ~15–25 items / week Every claim has a clickable source Open source ↗
Goal: a 5-minute read that tells an AI operator or investor where capital is moving, what's shipping, and which doors are opening — clearly, simply, and with sources you can click on every line.
